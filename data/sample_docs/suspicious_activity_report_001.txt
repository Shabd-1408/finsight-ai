INTERNAL SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORT (DRAFT)
Reference: SAR-2026-0143
Filed by: Compliance Monitoring Desk

Account Holder: Entity referred to internally as "Account Z"
Account Type: Current account, opened 4 months ago

Summary of Observed Activity:

Over the past six weeks, Account Z received eleven incoming transfers, each ranging
between INR 9,000 and INR 9,800. This pattern is consistent with structuring, where
transactions are deliberately kept just under standard reporting thresholds.

The account was opened in the name of a private limited entity that public records
show was incorporated only five months ago, with no identifiable business address,
website, or GST filings. Documentation provided at onboarding describes it loosely
as a "trading and consultancy" shell company with no clear operational footprint.

In three instances, funds credited to the account were transferred out in full to
two different third-party accounts within 24 hours of receipt, indicating rapid
movement of funds inconsistent with normal business cash flow cycles.

Two of the outbound transfers were routed to accounts associated with a jurisdiction
that has been flagged by the compliance team as high-risk due to weak AML
enforcement history.

The account has also shown unusually high cash-intensive activity for an entity
that claims to operate as a consultancy, which typically would not involve frequent
cash handling.

Recommended Action: Escalate to senior compliance officer for further review and
consider filing a formal Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) with the relevant
regulatory authority pending additional documentation from the account holder.

Status: Under Review
